# Property Tax — Indore Municipal Corporation

## Overview
Property tax is an annual levy collected by IMC on residential, commercial, and vacant properties. It funds infrastructure such as roads, drainage, street lighting, and sanitation.

## Due date and rebates
- The annual due date is June 30.
- Paying on time or in advance typically earns about a 6.5% rebate on property tax and a 6% rebate on water charges paid in advance.
- Missing the deadline attracts a penalty of roughly 1.5 to 2 times the outstanding amount, under the relevant amendment to Section 136 of the State Municipal Act, 1956.

## How the tax is calculated (approximate)
Tax depends on plinth/built-up area, the property's age (which brings a depreciation adjustment), its location/zone, and how it is used (residential, commercial, etc.). A commonly cited approximate formula is:

Annual tax ≈ Plinth area × Monthly Rental Value × 12 × (0.17 to 0.30), less about 10% depreciation.

This is only indicative — the official portal calculator gives the exact figure for a given Property ID.

## How to pay online
1. Go to the IMC website or MP e-Nagarpalika portal.
2. Under "Online Services," select "Property Tax," then "Pay Your Taxes."
3. Enter your Property ID; the system retrieves your property record and outstanding dues.
4. Confirm the amount and pay via UPI, debit/credit card, or net banking.
5. Download the receipt (also emailed/texted automatically).

## How to pay offline
Visit your zonal/ward office with your Property ID, ID proof, and any previous receipts. An officer calculates dues and issues a challan; pay by cash, cheque, or demand draft, and keep the stamped receipt.

## No-dues certificate
Available on request through the portal — useful when selling or transferring a property.
