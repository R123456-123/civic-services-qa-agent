# Birth Certificate — Madhya Pradesh / Indore

## Legal requirement
Under the Registration of Births & Deaths Act, 1969, every birth must be reported and registered within 21 days of occurrence at the place it happened.

## How to apply (within 21 days)
1. Apply online through the MP e-Nagarpalika portal or the national Civil Registration System (CRS) portal.
2. Fill in the birth reporting form with the child's details, date and place of birth, and parents' information.
3. Attach a scanned hospital birth proof and the parents' ID/address proof.
4. Submit the form; a nominal fee applies (registration within 21 days is usually free or very low-cost).
5. You will receive a confirmation once the record is registered.

## Late registration
If more than 21 days have passed, an affidavit and additional supporting documents are required, along with a higher fee, and in some cases approval from a designated authority.

## Downloading an existing certificate
Go to the e-Nagarpalika portal, select Birth Certificate → Download Certificate, and enter your application/registration number along with the child's name and date of birth to retrieve the PDF.

## Requesting a correction
If details (name, date of birth, parents' names, etc.) are incorrect, apply for a correction online with supporting evidence such as an affidavit, hospital record, or parent ID. Track the request status online, and download the corrected certificate once approved.

## Why it matters
A birth certificate is often the first legal proof of identity and is required for school admission, passports, inheritance claims, and other official processes.
